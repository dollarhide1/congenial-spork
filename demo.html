Nursing school is an investment in yourself. This planner helps you see the full picture — money in, money out, and every scholarship you're chasing — so the financial side feels less scary and more like something you've already thought through.

  1. Set your program length

    On the Budget tab, enter how many semesters your program is. Totals will scale automatically.

  2. Estimate costs by category

    Work through each section: Tuition, Books, Clinical, Equipment, Transportation, Living, and Personal. For each line item, type the cost per semester.

  3. Add your income & financial aid

    List every source: savings, family, job, scholarships, grants, and student loans. The summary panel updates live.

  4. Track scholarships separately

    Switch to the Financial Aid tab to track individual scholarship and grant applications. The included starter list has 20+ real nursing scholarships you can add with one click.

  5. Update status as you go

    For each scholarship, set the status: Not Started → In Progress → Submitted → Awarded (or Denied). The stats at the top show your totals at a glance.

  6. Filter to stay focused

    Use the filters to see only what's pending, what you've submitted, or what you've already won.

  7. Save, load, and export

    Save a .json backup, load a previous session, or export everything to Excel for sharing with family or a financial aid advisor.

  8. Revisit as you go

    Numbers change — tuition increases, scholarships come through, you find a roommate. The best plan is one you keep updating.

Pro tip: Apply to scholarships you think you "probably won't get." The smaller, niche scholarships have fewer applicants — your odds are better than you think. Try to apply to 2-3 per month, and always check each scholarship's official website for current deadlines and requirements.
